# Using X-ray Atlas

X-ray Atlas is built for day-to-day spectroscopy workflows, finding relevant
NEXAFS data quickly, validating its context, and contributing new measurements
in a reusable form.

## What the platform provides

**Search and filtering.** Query by molecule names and identifiers, chemical
formula, facility, edge, and experimental metadata.

**Interactive visualization.** Inspect and compare spectrum traces with
angle- and mode-aware plot tools, including an eleven-channel rail across
spectroscopy and optical-constant views; see
[measured and derived quantities](/wiki/nexafs/quantities) for definitions.

**Dataset quality signals.** Browse cards summarize spectral spacing, optional
signal-to-noise, and normalization anchors in one headline ring; see
[dataset quality metrics](/wiki/atlas/quality-metrics).

**Guided contribution.** Molecules, facilities, and experiments enter through
validated flows with metadata prompts; see
[contributing datasets](/wiki/atlas/contributing) and
[uploading spectroscopy](/wiki/atlas/uploading-data).

**Attribution and provenance.** Contributor records, publication links, and
citation metadata keep reuse auditable; the [API](/wiki/api) exposes the same
records programmatically.
