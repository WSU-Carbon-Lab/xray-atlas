<WikiLandingHero title="X-ray Atlas wiki">

This wiki serves two purposes. The **NEXAFS** section is a reference for the
spectroscopy itself, written for physicists, materials scientists, and chemists
who collect or reuse near-edge absorption data. It defines the quantities this
database stores and the conventions it uses, and it is maintained as a living
document alongside the catalog. The **Using X-ray Atlas** section documents the
platform, including the data model, upload formats, quality metrics, and the
public API.

</WikiLandingHero>

## NEXAFS

Start with the [introduction](/wiki/nexafs), then
[terminology](/wiki/nexafs/terminology),
[measured and derived quantities](/wiki/nexafs/quantities), and
[optical constants and Kramers-Kronig](/wiki/nexafs/optical-constants).
Chapters on detection modes, molecular orientation, and normalization are in
preparation.

## Using X-ray Atlas

The [overview](/wiki/atlas) covers what the platform provides. Reference pages
document [what the database stores](/wiki/atlas/data-model),
[uploading spectroscopy](/wiki/atlas/uploading-data),
[dataset quality metrics](/wiki/atlas/quality-metrics),
[contributing datasets](/wiki/atlas/contributing), and the
[API](/wiki/api).

## Citing and contributing

Hosted datasets carry their own attribution and citation metadata; see
[contributing](/wiki/atlas/contributing). Wiki pages are maintained in the
project repository and improve by pull request.
