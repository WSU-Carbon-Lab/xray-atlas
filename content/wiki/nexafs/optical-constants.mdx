# Optical constants and Kramers-Kronig

The absorptive and dispersive parts of the index of refraction,
$n(E) = 1 - \delta(E) + i\beta(E)$, are linked by causality. Measuring
$\beta$ across an edge therefore determines $\delta$ up to the accuracy of the
extrapolation beyond the measured range. X-ray Atlas computes $\delta$ from
stored $\beta$ on request and persists the result alongside the spectrum.

## The Kramers-Kronig transform

The real and imaginary parts satisfy the dispersion relation

$$
\delta(E) = \frac{2}{\pi}\;\mathcal{P}\!\!\int_0^{\infty}
\frac{E'\,\beta(E')}{E'^{2} - E^{2}}\; dE',
$$

where $\mathcal{P}$ denotes the Cauchy principal value. Because the integral
runs over all energies, the measured near-edge window is extended with
tabulated atomic scattering factors away from the edge before transforming.
The in-app implementation follows the piecewise polynomial method of
kkcalc [@watts2014], adapted for browser execution.

## Interpolation onto the measured grid

When the Kramers-Kronig evaluation grid differs from the uploaded energy
samples, $\delta$ is mapped onto the spectrum axis with modified Akima
(makima) interpolation, which suppresses the overshoot cubic splines produce
near sharp resonances
([OSA Continuum summary](https://opg.optica.org/osac/abstract.cfm?uri=osac-4-5-1497)).

## Recalculation in the app

The transform runs in the browser during NEXAFS upload when the contributor
opts in, or when an authorized user triggers recalculate on a dataset panel.
Values persist on `spectrumpoints.delta` with optional `kk_delta_metadata` on
the experiment row recording the run parameters.
