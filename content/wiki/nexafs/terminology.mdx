# Terminology and edge nomenclature

X-ray spectroscopy communities use several labels for closely related
near-edge measurements. This page fixes how X-ray Atlas uses each term when
indexing and describing datasets.

## Naming conventions

**NEXAFS** (Near-Edge X-ray Absorption Fine Structure) is the preferred term on
X-ray Atlas. It emphasizes fine structure in the near-edge region arising from
local bonding and electronic structure, and is the dominant label in soft
X-ray and surface-science communities.

**XANES** (X-ray Absorption Near-Edge Structure) is a widely used synonym in
XAS communities, more common at hard X-ray edges. In practice NEXAFS and XANES
describe overlapping energy ranges and interpretation goals.

**XAS** (X-ray Absorption Spectroscopy) is the umbrella label for absorption
experiments spanning near-edge and extended regimes.

**XAFS** (X-ray Absorption Fine Structure) names the structured modulation of
absorption versus energy above an edge, commonly partitioned into the
near-edge window (NEXAFS/XANES) and the extended oscillatory region.

**EXAFS** (Extended X-ray Absorption Fine Structure) is the higher-energy
oscillatory structure beyond the near-edge region, typically modeled with
different physics (single and multiple scattering of the photoelectron) than
core resonances.

**NEXS** (Near-Edge X-ray Spectroscopy) is a compact label used by some
groups; X-ray Atlas treats it as part of the same near-edge family.

## Edge nomenclature

Edges are labeled by the principal quantum number of the core hole. K-edges
arise from $1s$ core levels, L-edges from $n=2$ ($L_1$ for $2s$, $L_2$ and
$L_3$ for $2p_{1/2}$ and $2p_{3/2}$, split by spin-orbit coupling), and
M-edges from $n=3$. Soft X-ray work on organic materials centers on the
low-Z K-edges, carbon near 284 eV, nitrogen near 400 eV, and oxygen near
532 eV, with sulfur and metal L-edges extending the reach of the technique.
Catalog records identify the element and edge on every experiment so spectra
are comparable across facilities.
